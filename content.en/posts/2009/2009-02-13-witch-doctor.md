*Nine Stories, Season Six: Prologue*

*The Witch Doctor*

The room had plainly been left untouched for a very long time. It was tiny, containing only a bed one and a half meters wide and a cheap computer desk of the sort one could buy for fifty yuan. The gap between them was so narrow that only a person weighing sixty kilos could turn around in it. A thick layer of dust lay over the desk, marked by the tracks of beetles that had crawled across it. The computer itself was gone, leaving only the pale square where a CRT monitor had once sat for years. Above the bed was a round window half a meter across. At first glance it seemed to have been sealed over with black paper. Only when I shone my flashlight on it did I see that it was actually covered by a layer of black, furry vine.

The mattress, too, was thick with dust. There was no bedding, only a sweat-stained pillow with the hollow of a sleeping head still pressed into it. I measured the indentation with a tape: twenty-eight centimeters wide. It had been left by a thin bald man with a sharply pointed skull. Beside the bed lay three books: *Ancient Chinese Sorcery*, *Youyang Zazu*, and *A Study of Famous Chinese Things*. They were probably what he had been reading most recently. *Ancient Chinese Sorcery* lay open, face-down, across *Youyang Zazu*. I lifted it carefully. The open page concerned "sorcery for controlling human behavior and emotion: the making and efficacy of specialized love potions." The margins were filled with neat annotations in red ink. Two entries had been circled heavily: "grass that sways though there is no wind" and "peach-vermilion arts."

I pulled out my Nokia E71 and dialed a short number.

"Well? Find anything?" The man on the other end asked in an anxious voice.

"Damn it," I said. "This guy was a witch doctor."
