[Nine Stories, Season Six] Prologue

*Witch Doctor*

It was obvious that no one had entered this room for a very long time. The room was small, containing only a bed one and a half meters wide and a cheap computer desk worth maybe fifty yuan, with just enough space between them for a sixty-kilo person to turn around. A thick layer of dust lay over the desk, marked by the tracks of dung beetles. The computer itself was gone, leaving only the square blank imprint where a CRT monitor had sat for years. Above the bed there was a round window half a meter across. At first glance it seemed to have been sealed over with black paper, but when I shone a flashlight on it I saw that it was actually a layer of black furry vine.

There was a thick layer of dust on the mattress too. No quilt or blanket remained, only a pillow blotched with sweat, with the hollow left by a sleeper's head still sunken into it. I measured the impression with a tape: twenty-eight centimeters across. It had been left by a bald, gaunt man with a very pointed head. By the bed were three books: *Ancient Chinese Sorcery*, *Miscellanies of Youyang*, and *A Study of Famous Chinese Things*. These were probably what he had been reading most recently. *Ancient Chinese Sorcery* lay open face-down on top of *Miscellanies of Youyang*. I picked it up carefully. The page it had been opened to discussed "sorcery for controlling human behavior and emotion: the formation and properties of dedicated aphrodisiacs." The margins were crowded with red-pen annotations in neat small script. "Windless Swaying Grass" and "Peach-Cinnabar Art" had both been heavily circled.

I took out my Nokia E71 and dialed a short code.

"Well? Found anything?" the person on the other end asked anxiously.

"Damn it," I said. "This guy was a witch doctor."
