**[Tasmanian Tiger Reagent]**

Add 0.01 grams of Tasmanian tiger feces, 45 Chinese domestic-cat whiskers (none shorter than 2 cm), 0.3 grams of potassium dihydrogen phosphate, 100 grams of strong tea, and 0.2 grams of magnesium sulfate to 1 kilogram of orange juice at a concentration of 28 percent, then stir with a glass rod into a culture solution. Place the solution into a pre-sterilized ten-liter octagonal tortoiseshell-glass container. Heat with gentle steam sterilization, then cool. After 70 minutes, add 9 caterpillar fungi each about the thickness of a little finger. Under culture conditions of 25 +/- 2 degrees C, stir clockwise at 120 revolutions per minute while feeding sterilized filtered air at roughly 400 milliliters per minute per liter of culture solution, with an oxygen content not exceeding 45 percent. Culture for seven days total. After culture is complete, filter out the precipitated impurities, then seal the resulting filtrate into straight-necked indigo bottles of 100 milliliters each.

Color: sand-lizard green

Taste: faintly sweet

Use: the drinker will be surrounded by the rage of the Tasmanian tiger. Transformation effect uncertain. Felines have a 3 percent chance of transforming into a Tasmanian tiger.

Duration: 240 minutes

Side effects: unknown. Available from TRIR.

Source of formula: *Tihu Notes*

**Declaration**: tested and effective. Mango seems to like the taste very much. After transforming, she does not mind Tihu taking her out for a walk. Before transformation, please take care to ensure your own safety.
