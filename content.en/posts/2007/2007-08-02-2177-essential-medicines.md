**Generic name: Afluoxetine Green Tea Powder Capsules**

Former name: none

English name: aFLUOXETINE GREEN-TEA-POWDER CAPSULES

Pinyin name: AIFUXITING LVCHAFEN JIAONANG

Drug category: anti-radiation psychotropic drug

Properties: this product is in capsule form, containing pale green, white, or off-white granules.

**Pharmacology and toxicology**: Afluoxetine Green Tea Powder Capsules are a selective Psi-serotonin reuptake inhibitor (SSRI). They effectively protect the blood picture, raise white blood cell counts, and inhibit neurons from reabsorbing Psi-serotonin from the synaptic cleft, thereby increasing the amount of neurotransmitter available for actual use in the gap. In this way, they eliminate various disorders caused by low-level radiation, improve emotional state, and treat depressive mental disorders resulting from radiation sickness.

**Indications**: patients exposed to Grade I-III radiation who present depressive mental disorders, including mild or major depressive disorder, depressive episodes of bipolar affective disorder, psychogenic depression, and depressive neurosis.

**Dosage and administration**: oral administration of 20 mg once every morning. When necessary, the dose may be increased to 40 mg daily. Dosage and course of treatment should follow medical advice.

**Adverse reactions**: common adverse reactions include photophobia, vertigo, decreased libido, nausea, insomnia, and fatigue. In a small number of cases, intermediate radiation sickness may rapidly appear, accompanied by symptoms such as headache, hair loss, and skin ulceration.

**Contraindications**: contraindicated in patients with known allergy to this drug. Contraindicated for those exposed to moonburst or who have had contact with moon dust.

**Precautions**: because the half-life of this drug is relatively long, dosage should be appropriately reduced for patients with poor liver or kidney function, those who work long-term in outer space, and elderly patients. Use with caution in patients with a history of epilepsy and in pregnant or lactating women. Children should use it under medical instruction. If rash or halos appear, discontinue immediately and provide symptomatic treatment. It should not be used in combination with cola medicament; when necessary, combined treatment with anti-mutation drugs should be carried out in a fully isolated radiation-free oxygenation chamber.
