# Fenzita Translation Terms

## Scope

- This glossary is the working baseline for translating the `焚字塔` category into English.
- It is a production glossary, not a final literary verdict.
- Terms marked `pending` should be stress-tested in the first sample batch before broad reuse.

## Naming Rules

- Category name `焚字塔` stays as `Fenzita`.
- Do not translate `焚字塔` literally.
- Prefer short, stable English series names over ornate literal renderings.
- Preserve proper nouns from Chinese religion, folklore, and classical literature with pinyin when no clean English equivalent exists.
- For highly culture-bound names, prefer `pinyin + brief English clarification` on first mention, then shorten later in the same piece.
- For titles, prioritize readability over one-to-one lexical mapping.

## Style Rules

- Fiction:
  - Keep cadence lean.
  - Avoid pseudo-archaic English unless the Chinese is explicitly classical in register.
  - Preserve strangeness; do not over-explain inside the body.
- Nonfiction:
  - Prefer clear argumentative English over literal sentence order.
  - Break very long Chinese paragraphs when English readability requires it.
  - Keep named works in italics on first mention when appropriate.
- Culture-heavy prose:
  - First mention can use `pinyin (brief gloss)`.
  - Repeat mentions should simplify.
- Lists and vignette sequences:
  - Preserve numbering and compression.
  - Keep each item punchy.

## High-Risk Areas

- Daoist and Buddhist titles
- Semi-historical deity names
- Literary allusions with no direct English cultural equivalent
- Wordplay in series titles
- Local Chengdu toponyms and food references
- Culture-heavy food names that do not have stable English equivalents
